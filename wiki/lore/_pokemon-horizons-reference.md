# Pokemon Horizons Reference Note

Author-facing canon reference for *Pokemon Horizons: The Series*.

Purpose:
- preserve the major worldbuilding ideas Horizons adds to Pokemon
- separate hard canon from useful inference
- make it easier to borrow/adapt ideas while writing *Pokemon Amber*

This file is not trying to catalog every single episodic one-off. It focuses on the recurring cast, factions, places, objects, and continuity questions most likely to matter for Amber.

---

## 1. High-Confidence Canon Anchors

### Core premise
*Pokemon Horizons* is a world-mystery / journey story centered on **Liko** and **Roy**, rather than Ash. Official marketing frames it as a story about uncovering the mysteries of the Pokemon world.

### Main adventure crew
The **Rising Volt Tacklers** are the protagonists' traveling team. They move through the world on an airship called the **Brave Olivine** and are explicitly described as pursuing understanding of the mysteries of the Pokemon world.

### Main opposing faction
The **Explorers** are a named organization pursuing Liko and her pendant. This is a proper-name faction, not a generic profession label.

### Ancient backstory spine
The deepest mythic layer involves:
- **Lucius**
- **Rystal**
- **Gibeon**
- **Laqua**
- **Laquium**
- **Terapagos / Pagogo**
- **Lucius's Six Heroes**

This ancient expedition is not background flavor only. It is the central mythic-historical spine of Horizons.

---

## 2. Naming That Matters

### Lucius = Ancient Adventurer
Lucius is called the **Ancient Adventurer**.

This matters tonally. The Japanese title is **いにしえの冒険者**, which uses the fantasy-anime style word for adventurer.

This is useful for Amber because it suggests Pokemon canon is now comfortable using a more explicitly romantic / legendary adventurer framing for certain historical figures.

### The Explorers = named group
Lucius, Rystal, and Gibeon were the original **Explorers** as a specific group. Later, Gibeon's modern organization keeps that name.

Important distinction for Amber:
- **Explorers** (capitalized) can still exist as the Horizons-linked named group
- **explorers** (lowercase) can still exist as a broader trainer profession in Amber continuity

Canon is slightly awkward here. That is fine.

## 3. Main Recurring Characters to Remember

## Protagonists / Rising Volt Tacklers

### Liko
- girl from **Cabo Poco** in **Paldea**
- starts at **Indigo Academy** in **Kanto**
- partner Pokemon: **Sprigatito**
- pendant holder; pendant is later revealed to be **Terapagos** in dormant form
- descendant of **Lucius** and **Rystal**

- boy from a **remote island in Kanto**
- partner Pokemon: **Fuecoco**
- carries a mysterious **Ancient Poke Ball** connected to Lucius's story
- strongly motivated by the legend of the Ancient Adventurer

### Friede
- founding leader of the Rising Volt Tacklers
- partnered with **Captain Pikachu** and **Charizard**
- former **Exceed** researcher
- represents the mobile scholar/adventurer/professor lane

- not just mascot; active command presence aboard the ship
- helps define the crew identity

### Orla

- medic / healer
- partner **Chansey**
- reinforces the party-support / expedition-health side of the crew

### Murdock
- cook / logistics / morale support
- partner **Rockruff**

## Explorers / Antagonist network

- ancient companion of Lucius and Gibeon

### Diana

### Lucius and Rystal's descendants
- important because Horizons makes ancient adventure history genealogically relevant, not just mythically relevant

## 4. Lucius's Six Heroes

Lucius traveled with six famous Pokemon known collectively as the **Six Heroes**.

Known members:
- **Rayquaza**
- **Arboliva**
- **Moltres**
- **Lapras**
- **Kleavor**
- **Gouging Fire**

Important use for Amber reference:
- an ancient adventurer can have a legendary/mythic-scale traveling party
- old expedition companions can become region-spanning historical anchors
- the ancient-Poke-Ball / scattered-companions motif is very usable

Terapagos / Pagogo is separate but tightly connected to this mythic layer.

## 5. Key Objects / Forces / Lore Terms

### Liko's pendant
Initially mysterious. Eventually revealed to house / be connected to dormant **Terapagos**.

### Roy's Ancient Poke Ball
Ancient artifact linked to Lucius's story and one of the key drivers of the series mystery.

### Terapagos / Pagogo
One of the most important mythic beings in Horizons.

Functions in lore:
- key to Liko's pendant mystery
- tied to Lucius's expedition history
- crucial to what happened at Laqua
- linked to the sealing of Laquium

### Laqua
A legendary Pokemon paradise at the top of **Mount Pericarp**, described as the place where the land meets the sky.

It is not just a beautiful hidden place. It is the destination of the ancient expedition and the site of the central disaster.

### Laquium
English spelling commonly given as **Laquium**; Japanese **Rakurium / ラクリウム**.

Also called the **Treasure of Eternity** in some contexts.

Core traits:
- mysterious crystal/mineral substance
- extraterrestrial origin tied to a meteorite event
- located in Laqua
- stimulates life/growth but is also dangerous and corruptive when destabilized
- releases mist that can drive Pokemon feral / berserk
- tied to longevity in Gibeon's case
- later industrialized / exploited through Exceed and Strong Sphere development

This is extremely important for Amber because it shows modern Pokemon anime is willing to use a strange material / relic-substance as a major mythic and scientific driver.

### Strong Spheres
Later technological / corporate weaponization / applied use of Laquium-derived research.

Useful Amber lesson: Horizons is comfortable with a progression from mythic substance -> corporate R&D -> dangerous practical tech.

## 7. How Horizons Navigates the World

This matters a lot for Amber worldbuilding.

### Mobile-base structure
The Rising Volt Tacklers travel by **airship** rather than by pure road journey. This gives the show:
- fast regional movement
- a persistent ensemble cast
- a home base that moves with the protagonists
- a reason for support roles (mechanic, medic, cook, tech specialist) to stay present

### Mixed travel logic
Horizons uses:
- airship travel for major movement
- local overland movement once on-site
- sea / coastal / island access when relevant
- school, home, ship, wilderness, and city spaces all in one story system

### Navigation as a role
Dot explicitly handles communications and some navigation aboard the ship.

This is useful because it means Pokemon anime now supports the idea that an adventure team can have differentiated expedition roles, not just identical trainer functions.

### Ancient travel model
Lucius's expedition is older-school and mythic. It feels more like a long-range historical journey across regions with legendary companions and eventual route-making significance.

This is another major Amber-useful point: Pokemon canon now tolerates a distinction between modern expedition crews and ancient adventurer-legends.

## 8. Timeline / Continuity / Age Questions

## Is Horizons in the same world as the Ash anime?
Officially, the safest answer is:
- **it is not pinned with exact timeline math**
- but it is **not treated as completely disconnected either**

A Pokemon executive interview stated that **Ash is still in the world** and that Ash and Pikachu's journey continues somewhere in the Pokemon world, while Horizons focuses on Liko and Roy.

That means Amber should treat Horizons as sharing at least the same broad world-space unless there is a reason to diverge.

## How far after Ash is Horizons?
There is **no clean official date-gap** establishing exactly how many years separate Horizons from the end of Ash's run.

Do not overstate this.

Best practical takeaway:
- Horizons deliberately avoids depending on Ash-continuity specifics
- it leaves room for shared-world interpretation
- it does not give a clean timeline anchor like "X years after Ash became world champion"

## Ash's age in Horizons canonically
There is **no official Horizons age for Ash**, because he has not been integrated into the story directly.

If importing prior anime logic untouched, the default franchise habit is still "Ash remains effectively 10 / floating timeline".

But Horizons itself does not force you to keep that when doing adaptation work for Amber.

Since Amber already ages people up and uses a more grounded long-horizon structure, it is completely reasonable to age Ash up if needed.

## Liko and Roy's ages
Their **exact starting ages are not clearly confirmed** in the accessible official material used here.

What is safer to say:
- they begin as young trainers roughly in the Ash-age lane
- Bulbapedia tracks that by **Where the Adventure Leads**, they are already **one year older** than at the start
- the later **Mega Voltage** phase was announced as taking place **one year later**

So the current Horizons material is willing to move time forward more than the classic Ash anime did.

For Amber purposes, the important thing is not the exact number. The important thing is that **Horizons is willing to let time pass**.

## 9. Why Horizons Matters for Pokemon Amber

Horizons is probably one of the most important modern references for Amber because it proves Pokemon anime is now comfortable with:

- **adventure crews** with differentiated roles
- **mobile-base travel** rather than only road journeys
- **ancient adventurer mythology**
- **hidden paradise / lost-destination stories**
- **mysterious mineral / relic-substance lore**
- **cross-regional movement and discovery**
- **anime-original places** that matter deeply
- **proper-noun organizations with broad-sounding names**
- **letting time pass**

In other words, Horizons expands what feels "Pokemon-valid" for a story like Amber.

## 10. Adaptation Guidance for Amber

### Safe things to borrow / echo
- explorer crews and expedition identities
- ancient adventurer legends
- hidden destinations and route-finding
- material-substance lore like Laquium if transformed into Amber's own logic
- strong division between mythic past and modern institutions/corporations
- anime-original settlements, passes, or regions
- time progression and character aging

### Things to avoid copying too directly
- a one-to-one Lucius clone
- a one-to-one Laqua clone
- a pink-crystal substance that feels too recognizably Laquium
- making every ancient mystery collapse into the same family bloodline structure
- overusing capital-E **Explorers** as though the named group and the broad profession are identical

### Amber-specific use
Horizons gives Amber permission to do things like:
- make **explorer** a real trainer profession
- treat ancient route-finders as legendary figures
- add hidden settlements / region-links
- let special materials, ruins, or old expeditions reshape the present
- combine wonder, logistics, mystery, and danger in one narrative lane

---

## 11. Current Working Conclusions for Amber

1. Keep **Explorers** as the Horizons-specific named group if/when referenced.
2. Keep **explorers** as the broader Amber profession if desired.
3. Treat Lucius's **Ancient Adventurer** title as an important tonal reference, not a mandate to rename the whole profession.
4. Treat Horizons continuity relative to Ash as **soft-shared-world, exact distance unknown**.
5. Feel free to age characters up in Amber continuity. Horizons itself has already loosened the old frozen-time model.

# Explorers and Adventurers

## Overview

Not every trainer is a badge-chaser. Not every wilderness professional is a Ranger. Not every quest-taker is a gig worker.

**Explorers** and **adventurers** occupy the space between trainer, researcher, Ranger, relic hunter, and myth-chaser.

They pursue:
- Unmapped regions.
- Mystery Dungeons.
- Lost ruins.
- Ancient Pokémon migration routes.
- Relics and old Poké Ball technology.
- Strange energy zones.
- Legendary sightings.
- Places like Kitakami that are known only through rumor.
- Cross-region expedition routes.
- Stories other people consider impossible.

This is the cultural/professional category Don belongs to.

## Relationship to Existing Systems

### Quest-Takers

Quest-takers use Pokémon Center job boards to earn income and solve local problems. This is the broad base.

Explorers may take quests, but they are not defined by them. They seek the unknown rather than simply completing posted work.

### Rangers

Rangers maintain wilderness safety, enforce conservation law, manage route stability, and prevent ecological disasters.

Explorers often need Ranger permits, but they may also go where Rangers do not have capacity to investigate.

Relationship possibilities:
- Rangers rely on Explorers for reports and first-contact data.
- Rangers distrust reckless adventurers.
- Guild-certified Explorers can receive special permissions.
- Unlicensed explorers are treated as liabilities.

### Ace Trainers

Ace Trainers are elite battlers and crisis responders.

Explorers may be strong trainers, but battle strength alone does not make someone an Explorer. The distinction is field judgment, mapwork, survival, relic knowledge, and willingness to enter unknown conditions.

### Researchers

Researchers pursue knowledge, often through institutions.

Explorers may collect data for researchers but usually operate with more risk, myth, and field improvisation.

### Gym Trainers

Gym trainers belong to institutions.

Some may leave to become adventurers, especially after they realize that a gym only sees the world that comes through its doors.

Mary could eventually travel this road.

## Possible Explorer's Guild

The Explorer's Guild should be semi-formal, not a fully governmental agency.

It may:
- Certify expedition logs.
- Maintain maps of unstable zones.
- Register Mystery Dungeon entries and casualties.
- Mediate between Rangers, Gyms, and private patrons.
- Issue relic-handling permits.
- Train apprentices.
- Maintain old adventurer codes and ranks.
- Sponsor foreign expeditions.
- Keep records of failed routes, legendary rumors, and impossible places.

The Guild should retain romantic flavor:
- Field oaths.
- Old maps.
- Patronage.
- Expedition marks.
- Badges or seals.
- Unreliable legends mixed with real data.

## Adventurer vs Explorer

Possible distinction:

**Adventurer** is the cultural identity:
- Romantic, broad, questing, self-styled.
- Can include amateurs and dreamers.
- Don may call himself an adventuring knight.

**Explorer** is the professional recognition:
- Certified, documented, mapped, recognized.
- May require field exams, expedition proof, and guild approval.

Don might be an adventurer trying to become a recognized Explorer.

## Horizons Inspiration

Pokémon Horizons introduces relevant concepts:
- Lucius as the "Ancient Adventurer."
- Laqua as a legendary destination.
- The original Explorers as an adventuring group before later organizational corruption.
- The Rising Volt Tacklers as a positive modern adventuring crew.
- The idea that exploration can begin as wonder and become obsession, organization, possession, or legacy.

AmberTwo does not need to import Horizons directly, but the conceptual layer is useful.

## Don's Place

Don can consider himself an **adventuring knight**.

He is not merely doing a trainer journey. He is trying to live inside the older tradition of heroic exploration.

Possible goals:
- Earn recognition from an Explorer's Guild.
- Complete a foreign-region expedition.
- Find a Mystery Dungeon tied to Paldean knight lore.
- Track a relic or old expedition route.
- Prove that chivalric ideals still have a place in the modern trainer world.
- Bring home exploits to Paldea.

This gives him a reason to be in Kanto and a frame larger than badges.

## Oak's Place

Young Oak may have been an Explorer in all but name.

Before becoming Champion, he was not merely a battle prodigy. He traveled, crossed regions, found Dragonite Island, pushed into Kitakami's outskirts, and sought to understand Pokémon through field experience.

Oak's Champion career may be the institutional form of an earlier explorer's obsession.

Old Oak is what remains after exploration, Champion authority, war, and responsibility transform that fire.

## Amber's Place

Amber thinks she is starting a trainer journey.

Structurally, the story keeps pulling her into Explorer problems:
- Off-route incidents.
- Strange caves.
- Team Rocket operations.
- Rare foreign Pokémon.
- Mystery Dungeon-like phenomena.
- Kitakami.
- Mewtwo-related stones and legendary threads.
- Ceruledge pursuit.
- Mega/Darkrai-scale discoveries.

Don may be the first person to name the road she is already walking.

## Mystery Dungeons

Explorers should be the people most associated with Mystery Dungeons.

Rangers regulate the public danger.

Quest boards handle low-level fallout.

Explorers enter because the unknown itself is the point.

See `../mechanics/mystery-dungeons.md`.

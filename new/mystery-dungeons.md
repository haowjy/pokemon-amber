# Mystery Dungeons

## Working Definition

Mystery Dungeons are unstable wilderness zones where geography, Pokémon behavior, energy signatures, and spatial logic do not behave normally.

They may appear as:
- Caves that are larger inside than outside.
- Forest routes that loop incorrectly.
- Ruins whose layout changes.
- Storm pockets.
- Deep habitats with unexpected species.
- Places where ancient energy, legendary residue, or dimensional seams distort local ecology.

They are not necessarily common, but they are common enough that specialized professions, quest boards, Rangers, and Explorers know how to talk about them.

## Narrative Function

Mystery Dungeons let AmberTwo include strange Pokémon-world adventure without making every weird incident feel arbitrary.

They can explain:
- Why off-route spaces are dangerous even near settled areas.
- Why rare or foreign Pokémon sometimes appear outside expected regions.
- Why routes need Rangers and quest boards.
- Why Explorers exist.
- Why Pokémon Centers host rescue requests.
- Why Oak, Don, and later Amber can be drawn toward impossible places.

They also make "game knowledge != real wilderness" much sharper. Amber may know route names and encounter tables, but she does not know how a real unstable dungeon behaves.

## Possible Causes

This should remain partly mysterious, but possible causes include:

### Dimensional Seams

Ultra Wormhole-like undercurrents, spatial distortion, or long-healed tears in geography.

### Legendary Residue

Areas shaped by legendary Pokémon events, ancient battles, or long-term presence of powerful Pokémon.

### Aura / Bond Density

Places where human-Pokémon interactions, old oaths, mass grief, or long-term settlement history create unusual resonance.

### Terastal / Area Zero Adjacent Energies

Paldea-related energies could provide one explanatory family, especially for Don's background.

### Ecological Feedback

Large Pokémon populations, migration pressures, or post-war disturbances produce unstable behavior that looks supernatural but has biological roots.

## Types of Mystery Dungeons

### Stable Known Dungeons

Mapped, dangerous, but relatively consistent. Rangers and Explorers maintain route guidance.

### Unstable Dungeons

Layouts shift. Maps expire. Rescue missions are common.

### Event Dungeons

Temporary phenomena caused by storms, energy spikes, migration events, legendary movement, or human interference.

### Mythic Dungeons

Places that are half-history, half-legend. Kitakami's dragon gauntlet could sit near this category, though it may not be a dungeon in the mechanical sense.

## Public Knowledge

Most normal people know Mystery Dungeons as dangerous places from news, warnings, and rescue stories.

Trainers learn basic rules:
- Do not enter alone.
- Carry rope, light, food, and a beacon.
- Mark turns.
- Do not trust distance.
- Do not chase unknown Pokémon deep inside.
- Retreat early.

Explorers learn the deeper patterns:
- Dungeons have moods.
- Pokémon inside may behave territorially or ritualistically.
- Some paths appear only under certain conditions.
- Some items should not be removed.
- Some maps lie.

## Rangers and Explorers

Rangers manage public safety:
- Seal entrances.
- Post warnings.
- Coordinate rescues.
- Monitor ecological changes.
- Prevent reckless entry.

Explorers investigate the unknown:
- Map new zones.
- Record unusual Pokémon behavior.
- Recover relics when permitted.
- Search for lost routes and ruins.
- Take risks that Rangers cannot justify.

This creates friction and interdependence.

## Quest System Connection

Mystery Dungeons generate many job-board postings:
- `[Rescue]` lost trainer.
- `[Rescue]` fainted Pokémon retrieval.
- `[Item]` recovery.
- `[Investigation]` strange energy reading.
- `[Defeat]` aggressive Pokémon exiting dungeon.
- `[Escort]` research team field protection.

Professional quest-takers can work the edges. Explorers go deeper.

## Route 1 / Don Use

The revised Route 1 incident can use a low-level Mystery Dungeon or dungeon-adjacent zone without relying on "ancient Charcadet in a Kanto cave."

Possible setup:
- Don is investigating a strange cave report.
- Rocket is using the unstable zone to hide captures or search for valuable readings.
- Ursaring is captured or displaced during the operation.
- Teddiursa leads Amber into the outskirts.
- Charcadet is present because Don is present.
- The dungeon provides danger and atmosphere, but not the emotional basis for Charcadet's choice.

## Kitakami Connection

Kitakami may not be a standard Mystery Dungeon, but it belongs to the same family of "impossible places."

It is inaccessible because of geography, dragon territory, old seals, mythic history, and possibly Mew-related resonance.

Amber's eventual entry into Kitakami should feel like the matured form of the Route 1 pattern:
- weird place
- dangerous Pokémon
- old story
- human ignorance
- Amber pulled in because she cannot walk away

## Rules To Decide

- Can a Mystery Dungeon move?
- Can one close permanently?
- Can Pokémon be trapped inside for years?
- Can Poké Balls fail inside?
- Does time pass normally?
- Do Center signals work?
- Are there standardized Ranger markers?
- Are there known dungeon grades/classes?
- Are some dungeons legally restricted?
- Can Dungeon exposure affect evolution?
- Are Mystery Dungeons tied to legendaries or are they a broader world mechanic?

## Safety Rules

Possible practical rules:
- Never enter without a registered route plan.
- Carry physical line markers; digital maps may fail.
- Pair a Flying or Psychic scout with a grounded anchor.
- Do not split the party.
- Mark every chamber physically.
- If the way back changes twice, retreat.
- If Pokémon stop vocalizing, retreat.
- If compass/ball telemetry fails, retreat.

These rules can be learned by Amber over time and are perfect for Don/Oak/Explorer mentorship.

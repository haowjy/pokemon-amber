# Character Drive Spectrum

## Purpose

This file captures a planning lens for character motivations.

Not every important character needs an obsession.

Some characters are organized by obsession. Others are organized by ambition, duty, survival, attachment, avoidance, curiosity, resentment, or ordinary love.

The useful question is:

> What force organizes this character's life right now?

For some, the answer is obsession. For others, it is not. That contrast will make the truly obsessive characters sharper.

## Avoid Over-Moralizing Obsession

Do not frame obsession as simply healthy vs corrupt. That is too neat and too moralized.

Obsession is better understood as a force:
- It clarifies.
- It focuses.
- It distorts.
- It reveals what someone will sacrifice.
- It reveals what someone cannot see.
- It reveals what someone cannot live without pursuing.

Better fields:
- Object: What are they driven toward?
- Origin: Where did this drive come from?
- Discipline: What habits/practices does it create?
- Blindness: What does it make them unable to see?
- Sacrifice: What will they give up for it?
- Collision: Who does it put them in conflict with?
- Evolution: Does it deepen, break, transfer, fade, or mature?

## Drive Types

### Obsession

A life organized around a consuming pursuit.

Examples:
- Fuji: restoring Amber / the perfect family.
- Giovanni: power, Kanto nationalism, Mewtwo as answer to Mt. Silver.
- Don: adventuring knighthood, greatness, living by ideals.
- Later Amber: becoming strong enough that no one can steal her family again.

### Ambition

A desire for advancement, mastery, recognition, or status. Intense but still redirectable.

Examples:
- Erika: becoming Celadon's Gym Leader and making the gym worthy of the city.
- Mary: proving she belongs, then perhaps becoming authority without losing compassion.
- Kyle: proving the Fighting Dojo still matters.

### Duty

Responsibility that organizes life more than desire.

Examples:
- Oak after the war.
- Erika after becoming Gym Leader.
- Gardevoir if/when it chooses to hold the Kitakami seal.

### Survival

The need to preserve self or family.

Examples:
- Kaede saving Hiro.
- Delia protecting Ash and the life she rebuilt.
- Early Amber in the immediate post-Fuji aftermath.

### Attachment

Motivated primarily by bond rather than abstract goal.

Examples:
- Ditto with Amber.
- Teddiursa toward Ursaring / later Amber through debt and trust.
- Some team Pokémon whose central drive is relational, not ideological.

### Avoidance

A life organized around what someone cannot face.

Examples:
- Delia's inability to look at Amber without seeing Stephen burning.
- Oak's inability to explain the Sam/4Ever secret.
- Potentially Amber avoiding full emotional dependence after 4Ever.

### Curiosity / Exploration

Desire organized around knowing, seeing, mapping, or finding.

Examples:
- Young Oak.
- Don in adventurer form.
- Later Amber with Kitakami and other impossible places.

## Character Map — Current Draft

### Amber

Starts with survival, secrecy, competence, and plans. Not yet obsession.

Emerging drive:
- Keep promises.
- Protect Pokémon who choose her.
- Become worthy of trust.
- Later: prevent Rocket from doing to others what they did to Charcadet.

Amber's arc is not "was always heroic." It is the gradual acquisition of a consuming object through loss, promises, examples, and responsibility.

### Don

Drive type: obsession / chosen ideal.

Object: adventuring knighthood, greatness, exploration, living as if ideals are real.

Function: first positive obsessive figure Amber meets. He shows that passion can organize a life without becoming possession.

### Fuji

Drive type: obsession.

Object: resurrecting Amber and restoring the family.

Blindness: the living Amber in front of him is not his daughter; Delia has a life; others are not props.

### Giovanni

Drive type: obsession / ideology.

Object: Kanto power, Mewtwo, reversal of Mt. Silver humiliation, control through legitimate and illegitimate institutions.

### Oak

Drive type: former obsession transformed into duty, research, restraint, and mentorship.

Young Oak likely burned with curiosity/exploration. Champion Oak carried that into power. Post-war Oak is what remains after Mt. Silver and unification.

### Erika

Drive type: ambition → duty.

Object: Gym Leader title, but more deeply, making Celadon Gym worthy of Celadon.

Future question: once she becomes Gym Leader, does the role constrain or expand her?

### Mary

Drive type: ambition / service.

Object: proving she belongs in the gym system; later, learning what authority is for.

Potential future: Mary leaves on her own journey after Erika becomes Gym Leader, not to abandon Celadon but to become someone who can stand beside Erika rather than under her.

### Kaede

Drive type: survival / attachment.

Object: Hiro lives.

Potential later collision: Team Rocket may offer what legitimate systems denied her.

### Kyle

Drive type: inherited ambition.

Object: prove the Fighting Dojo still matters.

Growth: distinguish his own drive from Koichi's expectations.

### Sabrina

Drive type: ambition / control / mastery.

Object: psychic excellence, precision, elite path, proving Psychic Gym supremacy.

Amber as anomaly should bother her.

### Ditto

Drive type: attachment / agency.

Object: choosing Amber, choosing forms, becoming more than Fuji's weapon.

### Mewtwo

Drive type: autonomy / grievance.

Object: freedom from creators and a reckoning with origin.

### Charcadet

Drive type: becoming / chosen shape.

Object: what kind of fire it will become.

Don/Armarouge show one path. Rocket/Ceruledge force another.

### Teddiursa → Ursaring

Drive type: attachment / grievance / survival.

Object: missing mother, debt, later hardened loyalty.

### Vee

Drive type: survival / stabilization / revenge.

Object: escape pain, stabilize identity, choose form, possibly fight Rocket.

## Design Rule

Characters should not be flattened into "everyone has an obsession."

Instead:
- Let some burn.
- Let some survive.
- Let some avoid.
- Let some serve.
- Let some be hollowed out after the fire.
- Let some have not yet found their object.

Amber's education is partly learning the difference.

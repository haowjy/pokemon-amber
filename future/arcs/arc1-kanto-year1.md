# Arc 1: Kanto (Year 1)

**Region:** Kanto
**Duration:** ~12 months (flexible - these are story beats, not locked timeline)
**Detail Level:** HIGH (currently being planned)

## What Happens

Amber's first year as a trainer in Kanto. Fleeing Pallet with just Ditto, she survives through wilderness travel, quest boards, and reluctant partnerships. Economic struggle limits her team and progression. Learns to accept temporary help while maintaining independence.

**Major Events:**
- Pallet departure after Stephen's funeral
- Route 1 solo survival
- Trapinch rescue (with Flint)
- Viridian arrival & Trainer License
- Pewter Gym (Badge 1, Brock reunion)
- Economic struggle period
- Kyle partnership begins
- Celadon Gym (Badge 2)
- U19 Tournament (spectator/coach role)

**Ending State:** 2 badges, small team (Ditto + Trapinch), Kyle partnership formed, reluctantly accepting help isn't weakness

### Episode: Route 1 Solo (`episodes/route1-solo.md`)

**False Confidence:**
- Early Route 1: most Pokemon flee from her/Ditto
- "This IS like the games - Route 1 is safe"
- Overconfidence builds, wanders off main path exploring

**Getting Lost:**
- Forest all looks similar, can't find route back
- First night lost (not panicking yet)
- Adult brain: "I'll figure it out"

**Pikachu Nest Disaster:**
- Finds Pikachu colony, thinks "they're cute!"
- Underestimates territorial nesting behavior
- Pikachu swarm attacks
- Ditto protects Amber but overwhelmed by numbers
- Ditto faints - **learns fainted-can't-recall rule firsthand**

**The Rescue & Cave:**
- Amber scoops up fainted Ditto, flees
- Thunder Shock attacks grazing her (burn)
- Finds cave, dives inside
- Pikachu won't follow (Tyranitar territory)

**Meeting Flint:**
- Flint inside cave exploring for Larvitar
- Surprised: "What's a kid doing here?!"
- Helps with first aid, explains Tyranitar territory
- Temporary refuge established

### Episode: Flint & Trapinch Rescue (`episodes/flint-and-trapinch.md`)

**Discovery:**
- Amber and Flint traveling together (temporarily)
- Find Trapinch defending spot, won't leave
- Injured, exhausted, clearly been fighting
- Non-native to Kanto (someone's Pokemon)

**Team Rocket Encounters:**
- Butch & Cassidy claim ownership
- Battle: Flint's Onix + Amber's Ditto defeat them
- "Giovanni wants that Pokemon"
- Second attack: lure Scyther swarm
- Trapinch fights until fainting

**Amber's Choice:**
- Risks herself to rescue fainted Trapinch
- Flint provides cover, they escape
- Journey to Viridian Pokemon Center

**The Confrontation:**
- Registry reveals owner: Landon Ashford (age 17)
- Already replaced Trapinch with Rhyhorn
- Explosion: "He's weak! Two years, barely improved!"
- "If you think he's so good, you have him!"
- Abandons Trapinch publicly

**Abandonment Theme:**
- Trapinch: abandoned by Landon
- Amber: rejected by Delia
- Flint: abandoned his kids for Lola
- Parallel structure (not explicitly stated)
- Flint watches Amber protect abandoned Pokemon
- Mirror to his own situation
- Realization: "I need to go home"

**The Parting:**
- Flint gives/lends pokeball for Trapinch
- Returns to Pewter (family needs him)
- Amber continues to Viridian
- Temporary helper, not permanent companion

**Growth:**
- Accepting help kept her alive
- Her presence changed Flint's trajectory
- Temporary companionship is okay
- Being abandoned doesn't define worth

### Viridian Arrival

**Arrival:**
- Alone again (Flint went home)
- Processing: she helped someone without trying
- Still surviving, slightly more confident

**Trainer License Exam:**
- Written component (adult knowledge advantage)
- Practical battle (Ditto's versatility helps)
- Passes at age 10 (rare but legal)
- Can now catch more Pokemon (if she could afford pokeballs)

**Trapinch Official Catch:**
- Uses pokeball from Flint
- Second Pokemon registered
- Bond forming (shared abandonment trauma)

**Economic Reality Hits:**
- Pokeballs cost ¥30,000 (~$200 USD)
- Quest boards: ¥2,000-3,000 per mission
- Daily expenses: ¥500-1,000 (food for self + Pokemon)
- **Can't afford to catch dream team** despite knowing where Pokemon are
- Team will stay at 2 (Ditto + Trapinch)

**Travel:**

**Meeting Brock:**

**Community Offered:**

- "Not charity. Just... community. Trainers help each other."
- Amber learning: accepting help ≠ weakness

**Flint Payoff:**
- Flint returned home, helping with younger siblings

- Amber's inadvertent impact had real consequences

### Solo Journey Period

**Wandering Kanto:**
- Quest boards for survival (barely sustaining)
- Failed gym attempts:
  - Scheduling delays (2-4 week waits)
  - Cooldown periods after failures (1 week to 1 month)
  - Weak team (Ditto + Trapinch vs. stronger trainers)
- Walking between cities (weeks of travel)
- Can't afford flights (¥10,000 = week of quest work)

**Economic Struggle:**
- Research rare Pokemon locations
- **Knows where Gible/Larvitar/Bagon live**
- Can't afford pokeballs to catch them
- Frustration: adult knowledge ≠ economic power
- Team stays at 2 Pokemon

**Lessons Learned:**
- Gym battles require strategy AND strong Pokemon
- Money determines progression speed
- Most trainers fly between cities (walking is rare)
- Modern path = academy + flights (she can't afford either)

### Partnership Development Period

**Not Permanent Companions:**
- Different speeds/goals
- Cross paths periodically
- Kyle gets lost constantly (comedy, but also brings them together)
- Amber solo most of the time, Kyle appearances occasional

**Coaching Relationship:**
- Kyle asks for gym strategy help
- Amber analyzes his battles, suggests improvements
- "You're predictable. Every time your Mankey gets hit from right, you call left counter."
- Kyle learns: strategy matters, not just strength

**Collaborative Success:**
- Kyle wins gyms WITH Amber's coaching
- Wouldn't reach 8 badges solo (tactical weakness)
- Her strategy fills his gap
- His strength/resources enable execution
- Mutual benefit, genuine partnership forming

**Amber's Attempts:**
- Continues trying other gyms
- Fails most (weak team, scheduling, cooldowns)
- Economic struggle persists
- 2 badges is realistic limit for Year 1

**Growing Friendship:**
- Initially reluctant allies
- Gradually genuine friendship
- Kyle shares food/supplies (uncomfortable for Amber, but needed)
- Learns: accepting help ≠ burden

### Tournament Arc (Climax)

**U19 Junior League:**
- Requirements: 8 badges from 8 different gyms (pillar or minor)
- Registration deadline well in advance

**Kyle Qualifies:**
- 8 badges accumulated WITH Amber's coaching
- Collaborative achievement (wouldn't succeed solo)
- Represents Saffron Fighting Dojo (father's gym)

**Amber Doesn't Qualify:**
- Only 2 badges (Pewter + Celadon)
- Under 8-badge minimum
- **Spectator/coach role** - corner for Kyle

**Kyle vs. Sabrina:**

**Setup:**
- Saffron gym politics proxy war
- Fighting Dojo (currently official) vs. Psychic Gym (challenger)
- Both are only 10-year-olds in entire tournament (exceptional)
- Koichi's last attempt to prove Dojo's relevance

**Pre-Match:**
- Amber helps Kyle strategize
- Analyzes Sabrina's likely tactics
- Type disadvantage awareness (Fighting weak to Psychic)
- "You'll probably lose, but we can make it respectable."

**The Match:**
- Sabrina's psychic Pokemon + strategic mind
- Kyle's Fighting-types + Amber's coaching
- Type disadvantage too much to overcome
- **Kyle loses** despite preparation
- But respectable showing (not destroyed as expected)

**Aftermath:**
- Political impact: delays Fighting Dojo decline (doesn't prevent)
- Kyle learning: losing with dignity
- Amber: some battles can't be won (tragic realism)
- Sabrina introduced: true prodigy (what Kyle+Amber together are, she is alone)

**Character Growth:**
- Kyle values Amber's help (not threatened by it)
- Amber valued despite not competing herself (strategic mind recognized)
- Effort mattered even in defeat
- Found family solidifying (mutual support through loss)

## Themes Explored

**Independence vs. Community:**
- Proves she CAN survive alone
- But survival ≠ thriving
- Accepting help takes strength
- Found family through choice

**Economic Reality:**
- Knowledge ≠ power without resources
- Class determines progression speed
- Most trainers don't walk anymore (she's unusual)
- Pokeballs are luxury, not given

**Effort vs. Outcome:**
- Kyle's best WITH help still loses (Sabrina)
- Fighting Dojo WILL decline (predetermined)
- Effort still matters (character, relationships)
- Some battles can't be won (tragic realism)

**Worth Despite Limitations:**
- Trapinch: abandoned, slow, valuable anyway
- Amber: poor, weak team, strategic value recognized
- Flint: failed husband/father, can still return
- Being "defective" doesn't define worth

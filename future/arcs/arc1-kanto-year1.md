# Arc 1: Kanto (Year 1)

**Region:** Kanto
**Duration:** ~12 months (flexible)
**Detail Level:** HIGH (currently being planned)

## What Happens

Amber departs Pallet Town at 13 with Oak's blessing, a trainer license, starter funds, and Ditto. Her goal: 8 gym badges and conference entry. She acquires Charcadet and Heracross along the way. She meets Kyle Kong (14), and they form a peer-based partnership --- both pursuing badges, Amber providing tactical insight, Kyle providing muscle and (sometimes) direction.

The journey is properly resourced but repeatedly disrupted by weird Pokemon incidents that pull Amber off the gym circuit. Team Rocket operates as an escalating background threat. The gap between "normal trainer journey plan" and "weird stuff keeps happening to me" drives the arc.

**Major Events:**
- Ch 18: Licensed departure from Pallet
- Route 1: equipped but learning ("game knowledge != real wilderness")
- Charcadet acquisition (TBD: where/how)
- Heracross acquisition (TBD: where/how)
- Kyle partnership begins
- Badge progression across the year (disrupted by incidents)
- Team Rocket surveillance / interference
- Conference arc (TBD: does Amber qualify? How far does Kyle get?)

**Ending State:** TBD badges, full early roster (Ditto/Charcadet/Heracross), Kyle partnership solid, conference outcome resolved, setup for Arc 2.

## Early Journey: Route 1

**Amber is equipped but untested.** Oak's resources mean she has gear, pokeballs, supplies. But:
- Game knowledge != real wilderness
- Knowing type matchups doesn't mean knowing how to camp, navigate, handle weather
- Wild Pokemon behavior is more complex than expected
- First real humbling: being prepared on paper and unprepared in practice

This is NOT desperate survival. It's the gap between theory and experience.

## Badge Progression

**Goal:** 8 badges + conference qualification
**Reality:** Disrupted by incidents

Rough pacing considerations:
- Gym scheduling: 2-4 week waits for pillar gyms, less for minor
- Cooldowns after failures: 1 week to 1 month depending on gym
- Travel time between cities: days to weeks on foot
- Incidents pull Amber off-schedule repeatedly
- She's competent enough to win badges when she can actually challenge --- the bottleneck is TIME and DISRUPTIONS, not weakness

Exact badge count for Year 1: **TBD** (open question). Could be 5-7 realistically, with 8 as a stretch.

### Available Gyms (any 8 count for conference):
**Pillar:** Pewter, Cerulean, Vermillion, Celadon, Fuchsia, Saffron (Fighting Dojo), Cinnabar, Viridian
**Minor:** Psychic (Saffron), Ghost (Lavender), Bug (Viridian Forest area), others

### U19 Junior League
- Requirements: 8 badges from 8 different gyms

### Possible Outcomes (TBD):

## Character Arcs

### Amber:
- **Start:** Competent, prepared, untested. Game knowledge + adult mind + Oak's resources.
- **Early:** Humbled by real wilderness. Theory != practice.
- **Growth:** Building real relationships, handling disruptions, becoming a trainer through experience not just knowledge.
- **Pokemon bonds:** Each Pokemon trusts her for different reasons. She doesn't try to make them into something they're not.
- **End:** A real trainer, not just someone who knows about training. Defined by how she handles the unexpected.

### Kyle:
- **Start:** Reckless, tactically weak, overconfident. Age 14, Dojo pride.
- **Growth:** Learns strategy matters through Amber's influence.
- **Tournament:** Political stakes (Dojo survival), personal stakes (proving worth).
- **Loss:** Dignity in defeat. Effort mattered. Dojo decline continues regardless.
- **End:** Strategic fighter (still learning). Genuine friend, not just ally.

### Found Family:
- **Ditto:** Clone bond, loyal, intimate
- **Charcadet:** Driven, ambitious --- Amber respects its fire
- **Heracross:** Mismatch, comedy, warmth --- Amber lets it be itself
- **Kyle:** Peer partnership, reluctant -> genuine friendship
- **Oak:** Active mentor in background (not helicopter, but present)

## Themes Explored

**Plan vs. Reality:**
- Amber has a plan (8 badges, conference). The world doesn't cooperate.
- Story lives in the gap between intention and what actually happens.
- Not failure --- adaptation.

**Mismatch Identity:**
- Heracross: built for brawling, hates it
- Charcadet: not yet who it will become
- Amber: adult mind in young body, isekai'd soul in a clone
- Kyle: Dojo heir who can't strategize
- Theme runs through team and relationships without being heavy-handed

**Effort vs. Outcome:**
- Kyle's best WITH help still loses to Sabrina
- Fighting Dojo WILL decline (predetermined)
- Amber may not hit 8 badges despite competence
- Effort still matters for character and relationships

**Weird Plot Magnetism:**
- Amber keeps getting pulled into situations because she's good with Pokemon and can't walk away
- Not chosen one --- temperament and skill make her visible
- Incidents ARE the story, not interruptions to it

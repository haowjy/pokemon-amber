# Future Arc: 4Ever (Arc 4)

**Amber age:** 13 | **Status:** Seed. Key beats locked. Details TBD.

## What It Is

Arc 4 is the end of Kanto Year 1 — right before Amber's first conference. She has all 8 badges. She's earned her entry. Then the story happens to her anyway.

This is the arc where Amber realizes her game knowledge doesn't protect her. That failure is the pivot point for everything that follows.

---

## Entry: Teddiursa

Teddiursa faints in the forest. Fainted Pokemon cannot be recalled to their Pokeballs — this is a hard rule of the world. Amber refuses professional rescue services and goes back alone to find Teddiursa. That decision is what pulls her into 4Ever.

---

## Key Beats

### Ursaring

Ursaring — Teddiursa evolved, hardened from surviving alone — finds Amber during the 4Ever battle and saves her from a mortal situation. They reunite here. Ursaring becomes a primary team member going forward.

### Charcadet Stolen

The Iron-Masked Marauder (Team Rocket) steals Charcadet in the chaos of the battle. Amber walks out of the forest having found one and lost the other.

Charcadet evolves into Ceruledge under Rocket. It will not be recovered until Sinnoh (Arc 11). Amber enters her first conference without her ace.

### Sam

Young Oak — time-displaced by Celebi — is in the forest. Amber meets him. Oak has carried that memory for forty years. He doesn't warn present-day Amber about 4Ever because he's afraid of breaking the timeline — self-imposed restraint, not a rule she gave him.

---

## The Post-4Ever Call

After the events, Amber calls Oak. She's emotional. She lost Charcadet. She blames Oak.

Oak's position: in his memory of those events as Sam, Charcadet wasn't stolen. The timeline diverged. He doesn't understand it.

This conversation is the first time they speak honestly with each other:

- Amber tells Oak she's not from this world
- Oak tells her what he knows about the aura/bond system — that something exists at the champion level, that he's been researching it, that he doesn't know how to teach it

Neither character understands the timeline divergence. Neither names it. The reader infers.

This is the pivot in their relationship. Before: mentor and sponsor. After: something closer to equals carrying things alone together.

See: `wiki/characters/professor-oak.md` — the gap between who Oak remembered and who showed up.

---

## What It Costs

Amber leaves Arc 4 with Ursaring and without Charcadet. She enters the first conference with a hole in her team. The absence is felt through every battle.

This is also the moment she stops treating the world like a game she already knows how to win. The radicalization begins here.

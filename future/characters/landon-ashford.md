# Landon Ashford

**Trapinch's original trainer - Ground-type specialist preparing for Ever Grande Conference**

## Basic Info

- **Age:** 17 during Trapinch abandonment (Arc 1)
- **Training Duration:** 2 years (started at age 15 - standard start age)
- **Region:** Hoenn
- **Home:** Mining community (father owns mining operation)
- **Education:** Trainer school student (college equivalent with pokemon training + liberal arts)
- **Specialty:** Ground-type specialist (following father's mining profession path)
- **Goal:** Ever Grande Conference U19 (Hoenn)
- **Current Status:** Training in Kanto at Viridian Gym area

## Background

### Family & Profession

**Ashford Mining Company:**
- Father owns/operates mining business in Hoenn
- Mines various minerals, ores, rare stones
- Middle-class business (successful but not elite wealthy)
- Family tradition: Ground-type specialization tied to mining profession

**Type Specialization as Profession:**
- In this world, choosing a type specialty is almost like choosing a profession
- Ground-types = mining, construction, geology work
- Water-types = fishing, maritime, water management
- Fire-types = metalworking, energy, industrial
- etc.

**Landon's Path:**
- Expected to follow father into mining business
- Ground-type team = professional preparation
- Trainer school education = comprehensive training theory + liberal arts
- Goal: strong enough for U19 tournament, then family business with elite team

### Trainer School Context

**What Trainer Schools Are:**
- College equivalent that teaches pokemon training theories + traditional academics
- Liberal arts education: reading, writing, history, philosophy, mathematics
- Battle theory, type matchups, care techniques, breeding basics
- Does NOT teach you a specific job (that's apprenticeship/family business)
- Significant freedom compared to staying home, but less than full journey
- Expensive: tuition-based, middle-class and up

**Outcomes:**
- Trainer school graduates typically stronger than journey-only trainers
- Better theoretical foundation, structured learning
- Access to facilities, sparring partners, mentors
- Social networks (other trainer school students)

**Alternative Paths:**
- Full journey (traditional, harder, learn by doing)
- Regular college (no pokemon focus, for non-trainer careers)
- Neither (severely limits job prospects unless family business/connections)
- Significant portion of population doesn't have pokemon and doesn't need one to function
- Government pushes more people toward regular college (non-trainer) professions
- Culturally: journey is still the dream, but most don't have money/support/guts to last long

**Landon's Position:**
- Privileged to afford trainer school
- Has family support, resources, structured education
- Better equipped than most trainers his age
- Makes his abandonment of Trapinch harder to excuse

## Pokemon Team

**Current Team (6/6):**
1. **Sandslash** - reliable, evolved, strong
2. **Exploud** - evolved, coverage for Ground weaknesses
3. **Golem** - powerhouse, fully evolved
4. **Camerupt** - Fire/Ground, versatile
5. **Donphan** - heavy hitter, evolved

**Team Status:**
- "Rest of my team are top 1000 U19!" (Landon's words)
- Five team members are competitive, evolved, strong
- Trapinch is the weak link - unevolved after 2 years
- Team coordination suffers when one member can't keep up
- Pushing hard to get Trapinch "up to par" before tournament

**Post-Abandonment Team:**
- Replaces Trapinch with **Rhyhorn** (caught before Amber confronts him)
- Back to 6/6 team immediately
- Shows how quickly he moved on

## Kanto Trip & Viridian Training

### Why Kanto?

**Motivation:**
- Saw Giovanni's Flygon on television (Viridian Gym match broadcast)
- "That's what Trapinch should become!"
- Giovanni is the only trainer in Kanto with a Flygon
- Hoped Viridian area or gym had secret to Trapinch's evolution
- Desperate attempt to "fix" the problem before giving up entirely

### Viridian Gym Experience

**What He Expected:**
- Most gyms teach: leader present, giving advice, educational environment
- Gym trainers mentor challengers, explain techniques
- Community of learning and growth

**What He Found:**
- Giovanni is often missing (rarely at gym)
- Gym trainers offer battles for the sake of battles (no teaching)
- Cold, transactional, no mentorship
- Strange, suspicious atmosphere
- No help with Trapinch's training

**Reality:**
- Giovanni runs Team Rocket, gym is a front
- No one there cares about helping trainers develop
- Landon stuck waiting, frustrated, no progress
- Been in Kanto for weeks/months with no results

### Growing Frustration

**Timeline in Kanto:**
- Arrived weeks/months before Trapinch abandonment
- Training off-route near Viridian
- Pushing Trapinch hard to evolve before U19 tournament
- Other team members getting stronger, Trapinch falling further behind
- Emotional withdrawal: stopped seeing Trapinch as partner, started seeing it as problem
- "He has barely improved since I got him" (2 years of training, minimal progress)

## The Abandonment Incident

### Team Rocket's Plan (Author Background)

**Butch & Cassidy's Operation:**
- Team Rocket operatives notice Landon training at Viridian Gym area
- Spot rare Trapinch (only Giovanni has one in Kanto normally)
- Plan: steal Trapinch for Team Rocket collection/research
- Method: lure wild Scyther swarm into training area
- Expected: trainer flees, they wait for Scyther to leave, scoop up fainted Trapinch

### The Attack (First Time)

**What Happened:**
- Landon training off-route with team
- Butch & Cassidy execute plan: Scyther swarm descends
- Multiple fast Bug-types overwhelm the area
- Trapinch tries to hold ground (slow, defensive fighter)
- Trapinch faints from Scyther attacks
- Landon panics and flees with his other five Pokemon
- **Leaves Trapinch behind**

**Why Landon Fled:**
- Scyther swarm was dangerous (legitimate threat)
- His other Pokemon were getting injured
- Trapinch fainted (can't be recalled - worldbuilding rule)
- Would have to physically carry 33 lb Pokemon through dangerous area
- Chose to save himself and five Pokemon, abandon the sixth

**Team Rocket's Failure:**
- Scyther swarm attacks them too (didn't discriminate)
- Forced to flee before securing Trapinch
- Couldn't find fainted Trapinch in undergrowth afterward

### Trapinch's Loyalty

**After Being Abandoned:**
- Regains consciousness alone in wilderness
- Defends the spot where it was left (waiting for Landon to return)
- Believes trainer is coming back
- Refuses to leave the area
- Fights off wild Pokemon that approach
- Survives on battle energy + berries (hidden worldbuilding mechanic)
- Loyalty to a fault

## The Confrontation Scene

### Registry Discovery

**At Pokemon Center:**
- After Trapinch healed and stable
- Amber asks about registering/rescue procedures
- Pokemon Center scans Trapinch
- "This Pokemon is already registered to a trainer."
- Registry shows: Landon Ashford (Hoenn trainer ID)
- Center has contact information on file (he visited this Center before)
- Pokemon Center can't give out trainer information directly
- Center makes the call to registered owner

### Landon Arrives

**Initial Meeting:**
- Landon shows up at Pokemon Center
- Age 17, looks older than Amber expected
- Professional appearance (trainer school student)
- Team of 6 pokeballs on belt (already replaced Trapinch)
- Awkward atmosphere immediately

**The Awkward Exchange:**

**Amber:** "You're Trapinch's trainer? We found him injured off-route. He was defending a spot and wouldn't leave. We had to fight off wild Scyther to get him out."

**Landon:** (uncomfortable silence, looking away)

**Amber:** "Why didn't you put up a rescue mission for him? He was out there for days."

**Landon:** (shrugs, says nothing)

**The Silence:**
- Amber staring at him, expecting answer
- Landon avoiding eye contact
- Guilt hanging in air
- Flint watching, reading the situation

**Amber:** (seeing full pokeball belt) "You already have six Pokemon again."

**Landon:** (defensive) "I caught a Rhyhorn. I'm training for Ever Grande Conference. I needed a full team."

**Amber:** "What are you going to do with Trapinch?"

**Landon:** (long pause, silence getting to him)

### The Explosion

**Landon snaps:**

"He's a weak Pokemon that doesn't know how to think! I've been training him for two years now and he has barely improved since I got him, while the rest of my team are top 1000 U19!"

(Amber tries to respond with anime-like quotes about bonds, patience, worth)

**Landon:** (cutting her off, frustrated) "You're just a ten-year-old girl! What do you know about Pokemon training? I've spent two years, countless hours, resources from my family, trainer school education - and he STILL hasn't evolved! Every other Pokemon on my team evolved within a year. He's holding me back!"

**Amber:** (angry now) "So you just left him? He was defending that spot because he was waiting for you! He's loyal to you and you abandoned him!"

**Landon:** (voice rising) "I didn't abandon him - there was a Scyther swarm! I had to prioritize my team's safety!"

**Amber:** "You could have gone back! You could have filed a rescue mission! You just replaced him like he never mattered!"

**Landon:** (furious, losing control) "If you think Trapinch is so good, why don't you have him!"

(Throws hands up, storms toward exit)

**Nurse Joy:** (intervening) "Stop! This is a Pokemon Center. No shouting!"

**Landon:** (at doorway, not looking back) "I don't want him back. File whatever paperwork you need. He's yours."

(Leaves)

### Aftermath

**Amber Processing:**
- Standing in Pokemon Center, shaking (anger? adrenaline?)
- Flint's hand on her shoulder: "You did the right thing"
- Nurse Joy: "I'll process the ownership transfer. He'll need to confirm via the registry, but if he doesn't respond in 30 days, you'll get automatic custody."

**Trapinch's Status:**
- Recovered physically, resting in Center

- Still loyal to Landon despite everything
- Amber will have to tell him

## Character Analysis

### Not Evil, Just Normal

**Key Understanding:**
- Landon is not a villain in the traditional sense
- He's a normal trainer who treats Pokemon like team members (not deep bonds)
- Competent, educated, successful with five out of six Pokemon
- Fundamentally pragmatic: "This one isn't working out"
- Not malicious - just gave up when things got hard

**What Makes This Painful:**
- If he were evil, it would be easier to condemn
- But he represents NORMAL trainers who don't form deep bonds
- Many trainers view Pokemon as tools/partners for goals, not family
- His frustration is understandable (2 years, no evolution, tournament pressure)
- Makes the abandonment more realistic and more heartbreaking

### The Pressure He Was Under

**Legitimate Stressors:**
- Ever Grande Conference U19 approaching
- Family expectations (follow into mining business)
- Trainer school investment (tuition, time, resources)
- Team coordination suffering (one weak link affects all)
- "Top 1000 U19" status for five Pokemon (legitimate achievement)
- Trapinch genuinely holding back his competitive goals

**Why This Doesn't Excuse Abandonment:**
- Other trainers wait years for evolutions
- Could have released Trapinch properly through registry
- Could have filed rescue mission
- Could have found Trapinch a new home
- Chose easiest path: use Scyther attack as excuse, walk away

### The Core Difference: Patience

**Landon's Approach:**
- Results-oriented, tournament-focused
- Pokemon are team members toward a goal
- When one doesn't perform, replace it
- Pragmatic, efficient, emotionally detached

**Amber's Approach:**
- Bond-oriented, relationship-focused
- Pokemon are partners with inherent worth
- When one struggles, support them through it
- Patient, committed, emotionally invested

**Neither is "wrong" philosophically:**
- Many successful trainers don't form deep bonds
- Professional competitive scene is pragmatic
- But abandonment without proper procedure IS wrong
- And Trapinch deserved better than being left in wilderness

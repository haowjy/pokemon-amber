# Gym System: Pillar vs. Minor Gyms

**Detailed structure of Kanto's gym network and badge requirements**

## Pillar Gyms (Official League-Recognized)

### Current Kanto Pillar Gyms (8 Total):
1. **Pewter City Gym** - Rock-type (Brock)
2. **Cerulean City Gym** - Water-type
3. **Vermillion City Gym** - Electric-type (Lt. Surge)
4. **Celadon City Gym** - Grass-type (Erika)
5. **Fuchsia City Gym** - Poison-type
6. ****Saffron City Gym** - Fighting-type (Koichi, Fighting Dojo) - CURRENTLY OFFICIAL**
7. **Cinnabar Island Gym** - Fire-type
8. **Viridian City Gym** - Ground-type (Giovanni)

### What Makes a Pillar Gym:
- **League recognition** (official status)
- **City funding** (government support)
- **Badge counts** for Conference qualification
- **Higher prestige** (more respected badges)
- **Stricter standards** (harder to earn badges)

### Special Case: Viridian Gym (Giovanni)

**Surface Operations:**
- Legitimate pillar gym providing Earth Badge
- Giovanni as official Gym Leader (respected position)
- Conducts standard gym battles and certification

**Hidden Reality:**
- Front for Team Rocket headquarters
- Giovanni often absent (running TR operations)
- Gym trainers are TR operatives maintaining cover
- Cold, transactional atmosphere (no teaching/mentorship)

**What Trainers Notice:**
- Most gyms: teaching environment, leader present, educational support
- Viridian: battles for sake of battles, no instruction offered
- Giovanni rarely available (unlike other gym leaders who mentor)
- Suspicious atmosphere but nothing overtly illegal
- Experienced trainers feel something is "off" but can't prove it

**Why It Works:**
- Legitimizes Giovanni's public standing and authority
- Provides cover for TR activities in Viridian City
- Gym system is decentralized (no central oversight to investigate closely)
- Giovanni competent enough to maintain standards (badges are legitimate)
- Rare to have Flygon in Kanto (draws trainers seeking expertise)

**Story Relevance:**
- Giovanni's Ground-type mastery draws aspiring trainers
- Known for cold, efficient battle style

## Gym Challenge Mechanics

### Scheduling Requirements:

**Pillar Gyms (High Traffic):**
- **2-4 week wait** for appointments (popular)
- Online booking systems
- Priority for local trainers sometimes
- Can be longer during peak seasons (Conference months)

**Minor Gyms (Lower Traffic):**
- **Few days to 1 week** wait
- Often walk-in challenges accepted
- More flexible scheduling

### Cooldown Periods (After Failed Attempts):

**Strict Pillar Gyms:**
- **1 month cooldown** minimum
- Cerulean, Vermillion, Celadon, Fuchsia, Cinnabar
- Viridian (if Giovanni present - he's rarely there)
- High traffic, maintain standards
- "Come back when you've trained more."

**Moderate Gyms:**
- **2 weeks cooldown**
- Pewter (Brock is sympathetic but fair)
- Saffron Fighting Dojo, Saffron Psychic Gym
- "Work on your strategy and try again."

**Lenient/Teaching Gyms:**
- **1 week minimum** OR no formal cooldown
- Bug-type, Ghost-type, some minor gyms
- Focused on education, not gatekeeping
- "Let's review what went wrong together."

**Why Variation:**
- Prestigious gyms protect standards
- Teaching gyms encourage learning
- Traffic volume (busy gyms need breathing room)
- Leadership philosophy

### Battle Difficulty:

**Pillar gyms typically:**
- Scale to challenger's badge count (1st badge easier than 8th)
- Have multiple tier challenges
- Use stronger Pokemon the more badges you have

**Minor gyms:**
- Often have fixed difficulty (don't scale as much)
- May be easier overall (teaching focus)
- Some are HARDER (specialist/niche challenges)

## U19 Junior League Requirements

### Badge Requirements:
- **8 badges from 8 different gyms** (mandatory)
- Can be ANY combination of pillar + minor gyms
- Example valid set: 5 pillar + 3 minor
- Example valid set: 8 pillar (traditional)
- Example valid set: 8 minor (rare but allowed)
- **Cannot use same gym twice** (no farming one gym)

### Registration:
- **Deadline:** ~2 months before tournament (Month 10 for Month 12 tournament)
- Badge verification process
- Difficulty/prestige of badges considered for **seeding**
- More pillar badges = higher seed typically

### Tournament Format (Varies by Year):
- **Small year (<32 participants):** Direct bracket
- **Medium year (32-128):** Qualifying rounds → bracket
- **Large year (>128):** Mass elimination → qualifiers → bracket

## Badge Validity

### Carry Over:
- **Badges never expire** (keep forever)
- Can accumulate over multiple years
- Starting journey at end of season is FINE (badges carry to next year)

### Cross-Region Recognition:
- Kanto badges count for Johto U19 (and vice versa)
- Any Big 4 region badges (Kanto/Johto/Hoenn/Sinnoh) are interchangeable
- Encourages regional travel

## ACE-Level Conference (Separate from U19)

### Requirements:
- **ACE trainer certification** (elite rank, top 1%)
- NOT just 8 badges (need proven elite capability)
- Can handle unmapped wilderness
- Often involved in: exploration, route clearing, Ranger work

### Purpose:
- Path to Elite Four challenges
- Champion path qualification
- Most trainers never reach this level
- Separate competition tier from youth brackets

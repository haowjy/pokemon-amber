# Quarterly Conference System

**How the Big 4 regions coordinate tournament schedules**

## The Big 4 Regions (Japan Cluster)

### Geographic Proximity:
- **Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh** = "Japanese-inspired" regions
- Culturally/historically connected
- Relatively close geographically (established travel routes)
- Ferry services, flight routes, land connections (though wilderness dangerous off-route)

### Why Grouped Together:
- Similar governmental structures (gym council systems)
- Shared Pokemon species (migration between regions common)
- Historical trade/cultural exchange
- Post-war cooperation agreements (Indigo League confederation model)

---

## Quarterly Schedule (3-Month Rotations)

### Year-Round Circuit:

**Months 1-3: Kanto Conference Season**
- Kanto gyms most active (high traffic)
- Season culminates in Month 3:
  - **Kanto U19 Junior League Tournament**
  - **Kanto Conference** (ACE-level trainers only)
- Best time to challenge Kanto gyms (Months 1-2 before rush)

**Months 4-6: Johto Conference Season**
- Johto gyms most active
- Season culminates in Month 6:
  - **Johto U19 Junior League Tournament**
  - **Johto Conference** (ACE-level)
- Kanto gyms less crowded (off-season)

**Months 7-9: Hoenn Conference Season**
- Hoenn gyms most active
- Season culminates in Month 9:
  - **Hoenn U19 Junior League Tournament**
  - **Hoenn Conference** (ACE-level)

**Months 10-12: Sinnoh Conference Season**
- Sinnoh gyms most active
- Season culminates in Month 12:
  - **Sinnoh U19 Junior League Tournament**
  - **Sinnoh Conference** (ACE-level)

**Then cycle repeats (Month 13 = Kanto again)**

---

## Why Staggered Timing?

### Prevents Conflicts:
- Elite trainers can compete in MULTIPLE regional Conferences per year
- ACE-level trainers often do full circuit (all 4 Conferences annually)
- Youth trainers typically stick to home region U19 (but CAN travel)

### Creates Natural Story Rhythm:
- **Seasons** matter (not just arbitrary months)
- Conference anticipation builds over 3 months
- Off-seasons exist (calmer periods, less gym traffic)

### Travel Motivations:
- Follow the circuit (chase Conferences)
- Elite trainers: Kanto → Johto → Hoenn → Sinnoh → repeat
- Regional specialization (some prefer certain Conference types)

## U19 Junior League Structure

### Requirements (Consistent Across Regions):
- **8 badges from 8 different gyms** (mandatory)
- Badges from ANY region count (Kanto badge works for Johto U19)
- Under 18 years old
- Trainer License valid
- Register before deadline (~2 months prior to tournament)

### Tournament Structure:
- Varies by year depending on participant numbers
- **Small year (<32):** Direct bracket
- **Medium year (32-128):** Qualifying rounds → bracket
- **Large year (>128):** Mass elimination → qualifiers → bracket

### Seeding:
- Based on badge difficulty/prestige
- More pillar gym badges = higher seed typically
- Previous tournament performance (if returning)

### Why Multiple Regional U19s Per Year?
- Trainers can compete in MULTIPLE U19 tournaments annually
- Example: Compete in Kanto U19 (Month 3) AND Johto U19 (Month 6)
- Requires traveling + having 8 badges before each registration deadline
- Most trainers only do home region (travel expensive)
- Elite youth (like Sabrina) might do multiple

## ACE-Level Conference Structure

### Requirements:
- **ACE trainer certification** (top 1%, elite rank)
- NOT just 8 badges (need proven capability)
- Can handle unmapped wilderness
- Elite battle capability
- Often involved in: exploration, route clearing, Ranger leadership

### Purpose:
- **Path to Elite Four challenges** (Conference winners can challenge)
- **Champion qualification route** (traditional path)
- Most trainers NEVER reach ACE level
- Separate tier entirely from youth brackets

### Circuit Lifestyle:
- ACE trainers often travel full circuit (all 4 regions annually)
- Compete in Kanto Conference → Johto Conference → Hoenn → Sinnoh → repeat
- Lifestyle: constant travel, elite competition
- Prize money sustains this (Conference prizes are substantial)

---

## Badge Validity Across Regions

### Cross-Region Recognition:
- **Any Big 4 badge counts for any Big 4 U19**
- Example: 5 Kanto badges + 3 Johto badges = can enter Hoenn U19
- Encourages regional travel
- "8 different gyms" rule still applies (can't repeat same gym)

### Carry Over Years:
- **Badges never expire** (keep forever)
- Can accumulate over multiple years
- Starting journey near end of one region's season = FINE
- Example: Start Month 2 (Kanto season), Conference at Month 3 (not enough time for 8 badges), continue accumulating, enter NEXT year's tournament

## Gradual World Opening (Future Arcs)

### Currently Accessible (Year Amber Arrives):
- **Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh** (Big 4)
- Established routes between them (though off-route dangerous)
- Regular ferry/flight services
- Safe travel infrastructure (maintained by Rangers + ACE trainers)

### Not Yet Accessible:
- **Unova** (Western region, geographically distant)
- **Kalos** (different continent?)
- **Alola** (island chain, remote)
- **Galar** (far western region)
- **Paldea** (newest, unexplored)

### Why Routes Need Clearing:
- Wild Pokemon are DANGEROUS (Mt. Silver incident historical lesson)
- Off-route wilderness is deadly without preparation
- Safe paths require constant maintenance (Rangers patrol)
- New routes = years of exploration + clearing + infrastructure building
- This is why ACE-rank exists: elite trainers who CAN handle unmapped wilderness

### Route Clearing as Career:
- **ACE-level work:** Exploration, mapping, clearing
- Pioneering new paths between regions
- Dangerous but prestigious
- Government/League contracts (well-paid)
- **This makes becoming ACE MEANINGFUL** (not just strong trainer, but explorer/pioneer)

### Gradual Opening Timeline (Rough):
- **Current era:** Big 4 accessible
- **5-10 years:** Unova routes being cleared (occasional explorers)
- **10-15 years:** Unova accessible, Kalos exploration begins
- **20+ years:** Alola, Galar, Paldea gradually opening
- **This is NORMAL** (world exploration takes generations)

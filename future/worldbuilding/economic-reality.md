# Economic Reality: Modern Trainer Paths

> **Wiki promotion note:** The trainer path framework (class-based archetypes, social perception, why most stop at 2-4 badges) has been promoted to `wiki/lore/trainer-systems/trainer-paths.md`. This file retains character-specific positioning (Amber/Kyle/Sabrina), tournament dynamics, and detailed cost breakdowns.

**How trainers ACTUALLY progress in the modern era - class stratification through path differences**

## Modern Trainer Paths (By Class)

### Path 1: Standard Middle-Class (Academy + Flights)

**MOST COMMON path in modern era**

**Structure:**
- Live at home in one city (don't travel full-time)
- Attend **Trainer Academy** (paid instruction, like private school)
  - Battle tactics courses, Pokemon care classes, type matchup theory
  - Practical training sessions with school Pokemon
  - Liberal arts education (reading, writing, history, philosophy, math)
  - Study groups, mock battles, structured curriculum
  - Like college: broad education, not just vocational training
- Schedule gym appointments in various cities (online booking)
- **Fly to gym city** → take challenge → **fly back home same day/weekend**
- Repeat 8 times over months or years
- Never truly "journey" in traditional sense (gym tourism)

**Costs:**
- **Academy tuition:** $$$ (¥100,000-300,000/year equivalent, ~$700-2000/year)
- **Flight tickets:** $$ per city (¥5,000-15,000 each, ~$35-100)
- **Starter Pokemon:** $$$ (¥50,000-100,000, ~$350-700) - often family purchase or loan
- **Pokeballs:** ¥30,000 each ($200 USD equivalent) - expensive but manageable with support
- **Supplies/food:** Covered by parents (still living at home)

**Timeline:**
- Can accumulate badges over 1-3 years (no rush)
- Badges carry over years (don't expire)
- Can start near end of one Conference season, continue into next
- Typical: 1-2 years for 8 badges

**Social Perception:**
- **This is NORMAL now** (not walking journeys)
- Seen as responsible, practical, modern, safe
- "Why would you WALK between cities? That's dangerous and inefficient."
- Parents approve (kids come home every night)

**Examples:**
- Most 15-18 year-old trainers
- City kids with stable families
- Standard progression path
- Academy graduation → adult job or continued training

### Path 2: Working-Class/Loan Path (Self-Taught + Mixed Travel)

**Second most common - financially struggling**

**Structure:**
- Can't afford academy tuition
- Take out **starter Pokemon loan** (government program, high interest)
- Work **quest boards** for money (Rattata removal, berry gathering, etc.)
- **Self-taught** or learn from other trainers at Pokemon Centers
- **Fly when possible** (budget carefully, maybe 2-3 cities)
- **Walk when necessary** (to save money, takes weeks)
- Slower progression due to financial constraints

**Costs:**
- **Starter loan:** Repayment plan (¥5,000/month for 24 months = ¥120,000 total)
- **Flights:** Budgeted carefully (can't afford all 8)
- **Pokeballs:** Expensive luxury (¥30,000 = week's worth of quest income)
- **Living expenses:** Quest board income covers food barely
- **Gym fees:** Some gyms charge challenge fees (¥2,000-5,000)

**Timeline:**
- 2-4 years for 8 badges (slower due to money constraints)
- Failed gym attempts = lost income time (cooldown period can't work)
- Badges carry over (thank god)
- Often work between attempts to save for next flight

**Social Perception:**
- Respectable (working hard)
- But clearly struggling
- "Hope they make it"
- Some sympathy, some judgment ("should have studied harder")

**Examples:**
- Working-class kids without family support
- Orphans with small stipends
- Motivated but resource-limited
- Often stop at 2-4 badges (can't afford to continue)

### Path 3: Traditional Walking Journey (Rare/Old-Fashioned)

**RARE in modern era - seen as quaint or desperate**

**Structure:**
- Full walking journey between cities (ancient tradition)
- Camp in wilderness or Pokemon Centers (¥500-1000/night)
- Learn through experience, not instruction
- Quest boards for ALL survival needs
- Community of fellow journey-walkers (small, rare)
- Weeks of travel time between gyms

**Who Does This:**
- **Clan families** teaching ancient traditions (Kyle's Dojo path - choice)
- **Very poor** who can't afford flights or academy
- **Romantics/traditionalists** (rare idealists)
- **Mentored walkers** who choose the road despite having resources (Amber - Oak-supported but walks by choice/adventure)

**Costs:**
- Minimal money needs (survive on quest boards)
- But takes MUCH longer (time cost is huge)
- Dangerous (wilderness travel, wild Pokemon)
- No safety net (if injured or broke, stranded)

**Timeline:**
- 1-2 years for 8 badges IF focused and capable
- But most take longer due to survival needs
- Economic struggles extend timeline significantly

**Social Perception:**
- **"Quaint"** (if chosen tradition like Kyle - Dojo honor)
- **"Unfortunate"** (if poverty-driven like Amber)
- **"Reckless"** (if child doing it alone)
- Older generation: "Ah, like the old days..."
- Younger generation: "Why though?"

**Examples:**
- **Kyle Kong:** Dojo tradition (chose this path for honor)
- **Amber:** Forced into it (poverty + runaway)
- Some clan heirs maintaining traditions
- Very rare among modern trainers

### Path 4: Elite/Wealthy (Private Tutors + Full Support)

**TOP 5% - privilege path**

**Structure:**
- **Private tutors** (not academy - PERSONAL instruction)
- **Bred starter Pokemon** (perfect genetics, ideal temperament)
- Fly everywhere first-class (or family vehicle)
- Schedule priority at gyms (connections, money talks)
- Full support team (nutritionist for Pokemon, strategy coaches, etc.)
- Never worry about money

**Costs:**
- **Private tutors:** $$$$$ (¥500,000+/year, $3500+/year)
- **Bred starter:** $$$$$ (¥200,000+, $1400+)
- **Flights/travel:** Unlimited budget
- **Pokeballs/supplies:** Buy whatever needed
- **Everything else:** Money is not a concern

**Timeline:**
- 6 months to 1 year for 8 badges (fastest possible)
- No failures (excellent preparation)
- Can afford to retry immediately if somehow fails

**Social Perception:**
- Envied but respected (if talented)
- Resented if arrogant
- "Must be nice..."
- Expected to succeed (pressure)

**Examples:**
- **Sabrina:** Psychic Gym family wealth + Silph Co. backing
- Silph Co. heirs
- Old money clan families
- Future corporate leaders

### Path 5: Regular College (Non-Trainer)

**Exists but not story focus:**
- College for non-Pokemon careers (engineering, medicine, law, etc.)
- No battle/training curriculum

- Culturally seen as "practical" but less prestigious than trainer paths

## Economic Details

### Pokeball Costs (Critical):
- **¥30,000 per pokeball** (~$200 USD equivalent)
- **NOT ¥200** (like game prices) - actually expensive
- Catch rates are NOT guaranteed (realistic mechanics)
- Failed catch = lose ¥30,000 (devastating for poor trainers)

**Why this matters:**
- **Amber can't afford to catch dream team** (knows where Gible lives, can't buy pokeball)
- **Kyle can afford failed attempts** (family money covers losses)
- **Most trainers have small teams** (2-3 Pokemon common, 6 is expensive)
- **Most catches are situational** (rescue, encounter, not casual purchase)

### Quest Board Income:
- **Rattata removal:** ¥2,000-3,000 per mission
- **Berry gathering:** ¥1,500-2,500 per mission
- **Package delivery:** ¥3,000-5,000 (if you have flying Pokemon)
- **Pokemon Center assistance:** ¥1,000-2,000 (cleaning, basic care)

**Reality:**
- **Food costs:** ¥500-1,000/day (human + Pokemon)
- **Pokemon Center bed:** ¥500-1,000/night
- **Pokeball:** ¥30,000 (month's worth of quest income)
- **Flight ticket:** ¥10,000 (week of quests)

**For Amber (Oak-supported):**
- Oak provides starter funds, equipment, and supplies
- Can afford pokeballs, Pokemon Centers, and basic living
- Not wealthy --- working-class-plus, not elite
- Still uses quest boards for supplemental income and experience
- Pokeballs are still meaningful expenses, not throwaway purchases
- Somewhere between Academy path and Walking Journey economically

## Class Stratification Shown Through Story

### Amber (Oak-Supported, Walking by Choice):
- **Walking journey** (chooses the road for adventure, not forced by poverty)
- **No academy** (Oak-mentored, self-taught with adult knowledge)
- **Oak funding** (starter funds, equipment, supplies --- not shoestring)
- **Quest boards** (supplemental income and experience, not survival)
- **8 badges as goal** (disrupted by weird incidents/Team Rocket, not economics)
- **Year 1 roster:** Ditto / Charcadet / Heracross
- **Failed gym attempts** = setback, not financial disaster

### Kyle (Traditional Choice, Privileged):
- **Walking journey** (Dojo tradition, CHOSE this path)
- **Dojo training** (equivalent to academy, free family instruction)
- **Family money** for supplies (doesn't worry about pokeballs)
- **Can afford failures** (cooldown is time, not money crisis)
- **8 badges Year 1** (WITH Amber's strategy coaching + resources)
- **Full team** (can afford to catch/train multiple Pokemon)

### Sabrina (Elite Path):
- **Private tutors** (personal instruction, not academy)
- **Bred Pokemon** (elite genetics, perfect training)
- **Fly everywhere** (never walks, saves months)
- **Priority scheduling** (family connections)
- **8 badges Year 1 SOLO** (doesn't need coaching, self-sufficient)
- **Full support team** (nutritionists, trainers, strategists)

### Contrast Shows:
- Resources determine progression speed
- Talent matters, but money matters MORE for most trainers
- Kyle + resources + Amber's help = 8 badges
- Amber + Oak support + talent = 8 badge goal (disrupted by incidents, not economics)
- Sabrina + elite resources + talent = 8 badges solo
- **NOT preachy** - shown through experience, not told
- Amber's disruptions come from weird plot involvement, not poverty

## Why Most Trainers Stop at 2-4 Badges

### Financial Reality:
- Each additional gym = more travel costs
- Stronger Pokemon needed (more training time = less quest income)
- Can't afford to continue
- Get job instead (2-4 badges = employable)

### Practical Reality:
- 8 badges = years of commitment
- Most people have other goals (career, family, stability)
- Training is expensive hobby
- Conference qualification = top 1% achievement

### Social Reality:
- 2-4 badges = respectable achievement
- Can work Pokemon-related jobs (Ranger, breeder, Center staff)
- Don't need Championship to succeed in life
- Training is ONE path, not the only path

- Class dynamics still visible through the wider trainer population

# Trapinch

**Amber's second Pokemon - rescue bond with abandonment theme**

## Basic Info

- **Species:** Trapinch (Ground-type)
- **Origin:** Hoenn region (non-native to Kanto)
- **Capture Location:** Off-route near Viridian Forest/Route 1 border
- **Capture Timing:** Week 2-3 of Amber's journey
- **Evolution:** Vibrava (level 35) → Flygon (level 45) - very slow

## Capture Story (CONFIRMED)

### Week 2-3: Flint & Trapinch Rescue

**Meeting Context:**
- Amber has been solo 1-2 weeks, struggling with wilderness survival
- Off-route researching rare Pokemon populations (Trapinch/Hoenn migrants)
- Gets lost (inexperienced navigation)
- Flint (Brock's father) wandering down from Mt. Silver foothills
- Both hear commotion: Trapinch being attacked

**Visual Recognition:**
- Amber immediately recognizes Flint (Harrison family squinting eyes, darker skin)
- Adult brain + anime knowledge = "That's Brock's father. He shouldn't be here."

**The Rescue:**
- Wild Pokemon attackers (Raticate pack? Spearow flock? Multiple threats)
- Flint's experience + Amber's strategy + Ditto's versatility
- Team Rocket arrives (Butch & Cassidy) with fake Ranger credentials
- "THAT'S the one we've been tracking!" (reveals past attempt)
- Flint decisively defeats Team Rocket
- Trapinch stabilized through teamwork

**The Conversation(s):**
- Evening camp while Trapinch recovers
- Abandonment theme emerges:
  - Trapinch: abandoned by original trainer (slow evolution)
  - Amber: rejected by Delia (can't look at her anymore)
  - Flint: abandoned his kids to search for Lola
- "Just because someone ELSE decided you're not worth their time... doesn't make it true."
- Parallel recognition: all three wrestling with worth/abandonment

**Flint's Realization:**
- Amber's age (10) = same as his younger kids
- "Someone I cared about couldn't look at me anymore. So I left."
- Gut punch: his kids didn't choose to be abandoned
- Trapinch observation: "Still worth protecting anyway"
- Amber: "Your kids... they're still in Pewter, right? Waiting?"
- Decision: "...I need to go home."

**The Parting:**
- Flint gives/lends pokeball for Trapinch
- "Get to Viridian. Get your license. You're smart enough."
- Flint returns north to Pewter (purpose restored)
- Amber continues south to Viridian (with new companion)
- Temporary helper, not permanent companion

**Growth Moment:**
- Accepting help kept her alive (couldn't have won alone)
- Her presence can matter (changed Flint's trajectory)
- Temporary companionship is okay
- Being abandoned doesn't define worth

## Backstory: Original Trainer

### Timeline (~2 years before Amber)

**Purchased as Starter:**
- Hoenn trainer (~13 years old) receives expensive Trapinch from father
- Prestige Pokemon (rare, powerful final evolution)
- High expectations

**1-2 Years of Frustration:**
- Other Pokemon evolve quickly:
  - Taillow → Swellow (level 22)
  - Poochyena → Mightyena (level 18)
- Trapinch needs level 35 to evolve (very slow)
- After a year+, still hasn't evolved
- "What's wrong with it?"

**Kanto Trip (~6 months before Amber):**
- Sees Giovanni's Flygon on TV (televised Viridian Gym match)
- "That's what Trapinch SHOULD become!"
- Comes to Viridian hoping to learn from Giovanni
- Giovanni rarely present, delays challenges
- Trapinch STILL not evolving
- Emotionally checking out

**The Abandonment (~weeks before Amber):**
- Team Rocket grunts spot rare Trapinch during off-route training
- Attempt theft: lure deeper off-route, ambush
- Trapinch fainted in struggle (wild Pokemon interference, chaos)
- Trainer panics and flees (scared, overwhelmed)
- Grunts lose Trapinch (can't find fainted Pokemon)
- Trainer uses attack as excuse: "Those criminals probably took it anyway"
- Officially disclaims ownership
- Returns to Hoenn, buys new Pokemon

**Character Notes:**
- Not impulsive abandonment - slow emotional withdrawal
- Fundamentally viewed Pokemon as tools/status
- Rich enough to just buy replacement
- Crisis gave justification for pre-existing coldness

## Characteristics

### Physical
- **Small:** About 2 feet tall
- **Heavy:** ~33 lbs (deceptively dense)
- **Orange coloring:** Distinctive antlion design
- **Massive jaws:** Primary weapon and liability
- **Low mobility:** Can't run, limited jumping
- **Desert-adapted:** Struggles in rain/cold

### Combat Style
- **Defensive fighter:** Low speed, high power
- **Jaw attacks:** Bite, Crunch (devastating when lands)
- **Ground-type moves:** Dig, Sand Tomb, Bulldoze
- **Ambush predator:** Sets traps, waits patiently
- **Stamina:** Can maintain defensive position for hours

### Personality
- **Patient:** Will wait motionless for opportunities
- **Stubborn:** Doesn't give up easily
- **Protective:** Guards Amber fiercely once bonded
- **Trust issues:** Abandonment trauma, slow to open up
- **Determined:** Knows it's slow, doesn't stop trying

### Training Needs
- **Jaw control drills:** "Hold" (freeze), "Release" (relax)
- **Defensive positioning:** "Down/Bury" (half-shroud)
- **Sand manipulation:** "Circle" (controlled sand ring)
- **Warmth management:** Dry rest after rain, heat supplements
- **Grit checks:** Clean behind jaw hinge regularly
- **Calcium supplements:** Shell/exoskeleton health

## Battle Role

### Early Journey (Pre-Evolution)
- **Tank:** Absorbs hits, defensive positions
- **Area control:** Sand Tomb, Dig to control terrain
- **Type coverage:** Ground-type (complements Ditto)
- **Limitations:** Very slow, vulnerable to fast opponents
- **Strategy required:** Can't brute force, needs setup

### If Evolves to Vibrava
- **Mobility unlocked:** Gains flight
- **Speed improves:** Still not fast, but functional
- **New options:** Bug Buzz, Dragonbreath
- **Transition phase:** Awkward middle evolution

### If Evolves to Flygon
- **Powerhouse:** Dragon/Ground typing (excellent coverage)
- **Speed:** Finally competitive
- **Versatility:** Wide movepool, many strategies
- **Late-game asset:** Worth the wait

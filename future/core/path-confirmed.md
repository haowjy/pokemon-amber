# Path A: Age 10 Departure (Real-Time Journey)

**CONFIRMED** - The story will follow Amber's journey starting at age 10, experiencing the Pokemon world in real-time rather than time-skipping.

**Read `TONE.md` first** for story approach. This doc contains detailed structure - focus on story beats and character moments, not thematic language.

## Why This Works

- Readers discover the world WITH Amber (on-screen learning)
- Adult mind + child body tension maintained
- Survival stakes create compelling narrative
- Character growth shown through events, not told
- Found family formation happens organically
- Economic/practical realities of trainer life explored

## Modern Trainer Paths (Worldbuilding Context)

### Standard Middle-Class Path (MOST COMMON):
- Live at home, attend Trainer Academy (¥100,000-300,000/year tuition)
- Fly to gym cities, challenge, fly back same day
- Never truly "journey" (gym tourism)
- Badges over 1-3 years (no rush)
- **This is NORMAL in modern era**

### Working-Class/Loan Path:
- Can't afford academy
- Starter Pokemon loan (high interest)
- Quest boards for survival
- Fly when possible, walk when necessary
- 2-4 years for 8 badges (slower due to money)

### Traditional Walking Journey (RARE):
- Full walking journey (weeks between cities)
- Camp/Pokemon Centers (¥500-1000/night)
- Quest boards for ALL survival
- **Who:** Clan families (choice), very poor (necessity), runaways
- **Perception:** "Quaint" (if choice), "Unfortunate" (if poverty), "Reckless" (if child alone)

### Elite/Wealthy Path:
- Private tutors (not academy)
- Bred starter Pokemon
- Fly everywhere first-class
- Priority gym scheduling
- 6 months to 1 year for 8 badges

**Amber's Path:** Forced traditional (poverty + runaway)
**Kyle's Path:** Traditional choice (Dojo honor)
**Sabrina's Path:** Elite (Psychic Gym family + Silph Co. backing)

## Pokemon Team (Year 1)

### Confirmed Team:
1. **Ditto** (starter, failed Mew clone, perfect transformation)
2. **Trapinch** (Hoenn rare, rescue bond, late bloomer)

### Why Only 2 Pokemon?

**Economic Reality:**
- **Pokeballs cost ¥30,000** (~$200 USD equivalent)
- NOT ¥200 like games (actually expensive)
- Catch rates NOT guaranteed (realistic mechanics)
- Failed catch = lose ¥30,000 (devastating for poor trainer)

**Quest Board Income:**
- Rattata removal: ¥2,000-3,000
- Berry gathering: ¥1,500-2,500
- Daily food costs: ¥500-1,000 (human + Pokemon)
- **Pokeball = month's worth of quest income**

**Amber's Reality:**
- Works quests just to eat and sleep
- Can barely save money
- Researches rare Pokemon (knows where Gible lives, can't afford pokeball)
- Team stays small (meaningful bonds over collection)

**Contrast with Kyle:**
- Can afford pokeballs (family money)
- Can afford failed attempts
- Builds full 6-Pokemon team
- Resources determine progression speed

## Gym System & Badge Progression

### Pillar Gyms vs. Minor Gyms:

**Current Kanto Pillar Gyms (8):**
- Pewter (Rock), Cerulean (Water), Vermillion (Electric), Celadon (Grass)
- Fuchsia (Poison), **Saffron (Fighting Dojo - CURRENTLY OFFICIAL)**, Cinnabar (Fire), Viridian (Ground)

**Minor Gyms (Non-Official):**
- **Psychic Gym (Saffron)** - CURRENTLY challenger, will GAIN status in 2-3 years
- Ghost (Lavender), Bug (Viridian Forest area), Water-fishing (East Coast), Ground-desert (Route 11)

### U19 Tournament Requirements:
- **8 badges from 8 different gyms** (pillar or minor, any combination)
- Cannot repeat same gym
- Badges carry over years (don't expire)
- Can start journey near end of season (badges accumulate)

### Gym Challenge Mechanics:

**Scheduling:**
- Pillar gyms: 2-4 week wait (popular)
- Minor gyms: few days to 1 week wait

**Cooldown After Failure:**
- Strict gyms: 1 month (Cerulean, Vermillion, Celadon, Fuchsia, Cinnabar)
- Moderate gyms: 2 weeks (Pewter, Saffron gyms)
- Lenient gyms: 1 week (Bug, Ghost, some minor)

**Why Amber Has Only 2 Badges:**
- Walking takes weeks between cities
- Scheduling delays (weeks of wait time)
- Failed attempts = cooldown periods
- Quest boards for survival (time away from training)
- Can't afford flights (save months of travel time)

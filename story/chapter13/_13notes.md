## Ditto's Behavior — The Hyper Beam Sequence

Ditto does not cleanly refuse. It fires. The sequence matters:

1. **Dragon Rage on Poliwag** — complies fully, no hesitation
2. **Hyper Beam on Stephen** — two beats of visible resistance (coils backward, eyes meet Stephen's, scales trembling, eyes squeezed shut), then fires — but deliberately holds back. Fuji walks up to Stephen's crumpled body and immediately says *"Why did you hold back?"* — the attack was weakened or misdirected. Stephen is alive enough that Fuji needs to order a follow-up.
3. **"Finish them. Bite."** — refuses completely. Form starts dissolving back to pink.
4. Fuji recalls it as useless and sends out Magmar instead. Magmar completes the kill without hesitation.

Ditto tried to thread an impossible needle — fire but not kill — and it didn't work. Stephen still dies. The refusal wasn't enough.

This is not "hardwired moral resistance." It's a gradual collapse: each command pushes further than the last. It completes attacks on Pokemon, hesitates on lethal attacks against humans, and finally breaks at execution of the defenseless.

By ch15-16, with Amber present, it refuses entirely from the start. That escalation is the arc.
